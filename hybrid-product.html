Smart Soles is a smart wearable insole that dynamically tracks and helps to improve balance for aging individuals. It provides a preventative and long-term approach to personal healthcare, and decreases the worries of healthcare professionals and relatives by discreetly capturing balance progress.

Background

Smart Soles was born out of a tech venture incubator capstone program, after discovering that falls are a leading cause of injuries and deaths in seniors.

As the product designer of the team, I touched on all aspects of the product from ideation to implementation for the hardware, software and business components of the entire product cycle.

Check out our product video!

The context

Did you know that in the United States, 1 senior dies every 19 minutes as a result of a fall? Key causes of falls include muscular deterioration, inactivity, and a lapse of accurate judgement. While other factors are also important in how balance is maintained (eg. sensory perception), we are focusing on improving physical health and consumer accessibility to personal health.

The design challenge

How can we create a product that is easy to use, senior-oriented, and shows + improve balance progress over time?

Research

User Interviews

My team and I were able to conduct over 100+ interviews with users from this ecosystem, including: seniors, doctors, physiotherapists, adult children (30-50s years old) and grandchildren (<30 years old).

This allowed us to understand more holistically of the existing experiences, feelings and frustrations from all those involved in this ecosystem. It also allowed us to validate our product scope and user segments.

Observations

After observing elderly individuals at coffee shops, senior centers, community centers, etc., we were able to take note of certain patterns and habits that the elderly had, such as a dependency on their walking devices and large fonts for their phones.

Competitive analysis

I decided to place an emphasis on the Fitbit app, the Apple Health app and Google Fit for competitive analysis, since they hold a strong presence in the wearable industry.

Product scope

The whitespace analysis of competing smart insoles demonstrates how our technical features differentiate, while the petal diagram indicates how our product will provide value in the current market environment.
